About

Hi, I'm Muhammad Amru Al-Amin, a graduate student in the Department of Physics at National Chung Hsing University in Taiwan, fortunate to be advised by Shin-Liang Chen. My research broadly focuses on quantum information theory, with a particular interest in fundamental quantum correlations, often motivated by their applications in a device-independent framework.

Currently, I am a member of the NCHU Quantum Information Group, working on problems related to Bell's nonlocality and measurement incompatibility in multipartite systems (quantum networks), using semidefinite programming and its relaxations. In 2024, I completed my master’s degree in this group with a thesis on bounding quantum correlations in bilocal networks via semidefinite relaxations .

I earned my undergraduate degree in the Department of Physics at Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember in Indonesia, where I worked with Agus Purwanto on quantum computing. My thesis focused on applying the Bernstein-Vazirani algorithm to a nuclear magnetic resonance-based quantum computer model, completed in 2021.
